# Scam Shield Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: July 18, 2026**

Scam Shield is a browser extension developed by Arthiva Labs. This Privacy Policy explains how Scam Shield handles information when you use the extension.

## 1. How Scam Shield Works

Scam Shield analyzes certain characteristics of the webpage you choose to scan in order to identify common website trust and scam risk signals.

A scan only runs when you choose to initiate it using the Scam Shield extension.

The extension may analyze technical information about the current webpage, including:

- The website URL and domain name
- Whether the website uses HTTPS
- The number of forms detected on the page
- The number of links and external links
- The presence of password input fields
- The presence of payment-related input fields
- Other technical webpage characteristics used by Scam Shield's rule-based risk analysis

This information is used to generate a trust score and explain potential risk signals.

## 2. Personal Information

Scam Shield does not intentionally collect the personal information you enter into websites.

Scam Shield does not intentionally collect or store:

- Passwords
- Credit or debit card numbers
- Banking credentials
- Messages
- Emails
- Form contents entered by users

The extension may detect the presence of certain types of webpage fields, such as password or payment-related fields, as part of its risk analysis. It does not need to read the values entered into those fields for this purpose.

## 3. Website Scan Data

The current version of Scam Shield performs rule-based website analysis through the browser extension.

Scam Shield may inspect technical characteristics of the active webpage when you explicitly initiate a scan.

Scam Shield does not continuously scan your browsing activity in the background.

## 4. Local Storage

Scam Shield may use Chrome's local extension storage to save limited extension-related information, such as:

- Daily scan usage
- Free or Pro plan status
- Extension preferences

This information is used to operate Scam Shield features.

## 5. Payments

Scam Shield may offer optional paid features through Scam Shield Pro.

Payments may be processed by third-party payment providers. Payment information is handled by the applicable payment provider and is subject to that provider's privacy and security practices.

Scam Shield and Arthiva Labs do not directly store complete payment card details.

## 6. Data Sharing

Scam Shield does not sell users' personal information.

Information may be processed by service providers when necessary to operate specific features, such as payment processing. Any such processing will be limited to what is required to provide the relevant service.

## 7. Browser Permissions

Scam Shield requests browser permissions required to provide its functionality.

Depending on the version of the extension, these permissions may include access required to:

- Identify the active browser tab
- Analyze the webpage you choose to scan
- Run the website scanning functionality
- Store extension settings and usage information locally

Scam Shield uses these permissions to provide its stated functionality.

## 8. Security and Accuracy

Scam Shield is designed to help users identify common website trust and scam risk signals.

However, no automated or rule-based system can guarantee that a website is safe or unsafe.

A high trust score does not guarantee that a website is legitimate, secure, or free from fraud. Similarly, a lower trust score does not necessarily mean that a website is fraudulent.

Users should use Scam Shield as an informational safety tool and exercise their own judgment before sharing personal information, making payments, downloading files, or interacting with unfamiliar websites.

## 9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated as Scam Shield develops or as its features and data practices change.

The latest version of this Privacy Policy will include the date of the most recent update.
